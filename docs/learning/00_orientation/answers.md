# Answers — Orientation

1. A regime is a partition label (tenant/source/category) used to group items for accounting and comparison.
2. CM ranks regimes; AS lists retained items with shares; TR is the decision/provenance log; EB records discarded mass by reason/regime.
3. So discarded mass is never hidden; it remains auditable and attributable.
4. How many items are needed to account for 90% of retained mass; fewer means more concentration.
5. Global share is relative to the whole dataset; local share is relative to the item’s regime.
6. To produce reproducible, audit-first artifacts that explain where mass concentrates and what was discarded under normalization and retention.
